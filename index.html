The hidden curriculum of the semester: BU has deadlines that can seriously affect your financial aid, grades, and housing — and nobody sends you a big reminder. Here's what to watch for, month by month. Always check the official BU Academic Calendar for exact dates each semester: bu.edu/academics/calendar
Fall semester
  • September
    SPLASH — BU's big student activities fair happens early in the fall semester. This is your best shot to find clubs, orgs, and communities that feel like you. Even if you're not a joiner, just walking through is worth it.

    Start visiting professors during office hours early — before you need anything. It's way less awkward than showing up when you're already struggling. Office hours are free, drop-in time with your professor — you don't need an appointment or a specific question to go.
  • October
    Check your grades so far — have you gotten feedback on assignments? Mid-terms are coming. This is a good time to flag anything that isn't going well before it gets worse.
    ⚠️ Early fall: Check the BU calendar for the last day to drop a course WITHOUT a "W" grade
    ⚠️ Check the BU calendar for the spring study abroad application deadline

    A "W" on your transcript means you withdrew from the class. It doesn't hurt your GPA, but too many can raise questions. Dropping before the deadline means no record of it at all. Talk to your advisor before dropping any course.
  • November
    Course registration happens in November. You'll need to meet with your academic advisor before you can register — this is required, not optional. Schedule that meeting early because advisor slots fill up fast.
    ⚠️ Mid-to-late November: Last day to drop a course with a "W" grade — check the BU calendar
    Summer internship applications start opening in November and December. The best opportunities close early — don't wait until spring to start looking.
  • December
    Finals season. Build a study schedule at least two weeks before exams start — cramming the night before rarely works.
    ⚠️ Book travel home early — prices spike significantly as the holidays approach
    Dorm students must vacate for winter break. If you can't go home, BU has options — contact the Dean of Students Office. If home isn't a safe place, reach out to them confidentially.
Spring semester
  • January
    BU holds a winter activities fair early in January — a great chance to find clubs if you missed SPLASH or want to try something new. January is also when many summer on-campus job applications open, including orientation positions. If you want to stay in Boston over the summer, apply early.
    ⚠️ RA applications for the following academic year typically open in January
  • February
    Housing portal opens in late January or early February. If you want to live on campus next year, you must complete the application and pay a deposit to receive a housing selection number. Missing this window means starting from the back of the line.
    ⚠️ Check the BU calendar: last day to drop a course without a "W" grade is in February
  • March
    Housing selection happens in March — seniors choose first, then juniors, then sophomores. Gender-affirming and gender-neutral housing options have separate deadlines; check the BU Housing site.

    Mid-terms are typically in March. Check your grades and get ahead of anything that's slipping — there's still time to turn things around.
    ⚠️ Check the BU calendar for study abroad application deadlines (summer and fall programs)
  • April
    ⚠️ FAFSA renewal priority deadline is in early April — if you receive financial aid, this is critical
    ⚠️ Check the BU calendar: last day to drop with a "W" or designate a course pass/fail

    The FAFSA is the federal form that determines your financial aid each year. You must renew it annually — it doesn't carry over automatically. Go to studentaid.gov and log in with your FSA ID. Missing this deadline can affect your aid for next year.
  • May
    Reading period and final exams happen in early May. Dining hall service ends at the close of finals — make meal and travel plans accordingly. Book travel home early; prices go up fast near Commencement.
    ⚠️ Check the BU calendar for move-out deadlines — they differ for degree and non-degree students
Satisfactory Academic Progress (SAP) — protect your financial aid

To keep your financial aid, you must pass at least 75% of the credits you attempt and maintain at least a 2.0 cumulative GPA each year. This applies to CASS Scholars and most students receiving aid.

If you fall below SAP, your aid can be suspended — but you can appeal. The appeal asks you to explain what happened and what you'll do differently. You can include letters from doctors, professors, or advisors. If you're worried about your GPA or credits, talk to your advisor now, not at the end of the semester.

Learn more about SAP and how to appeal → bu.edu/finaid

These resources exist because students need them. Using them isn't a sign you're struggling — it's a sign you're smart enough to use what's available. Every student at BU has access to all of these, free.
Food & basic needs
BU Food Pantry
Free food, toiletries, and menstrual products. Order online and pick up discreetly on the Charles River or Medical campuses. No questions asked.
bu.edu/studentwellbeing/bu-food-pantry
Terrier Meal Share
Need dining hall meals? The Dean of Students Office can connect you. Other students donate unused guest meals, and BU matches them. No cost to you.
bu.edu/dos/terrier-meal-share
Financial Emergency Help
Students have gotten help with laptop loaners, emergency housing, and flights home for family emergencies. Contact the Dean of Students Office.
