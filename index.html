The hidden curriculum of the semester: BU has deadlines that can seriously affect your financial aid, grades, and housing — and nobody sends you a big reminder. Here's what to watch for, month by month.
Fall semester
  • September
    SPLASH — BU's big student activities fair happens early in the fall. Your best shot to find clubs, orgs, and communities that feel like you. Even if you're not a joiner, just walking through is worth it.

    Start visiting professors during office hours early — before you need anything. Office hours are free, drop-in time with your professor. You don't need an appointment or a specific question. It's way less awkward than showing up when you're already struggling.
  • October
    Check your grades so far — have you gotten feedback on assignments? Mid-terms are coming. This is a good time to flag anything that isn't going well before it gets worse.
    ⚠️ Check the BU calendar: last day to drop a course WITHOUT a "W" grade
    ⚠️ Check the BU calendar: spring study abroad application deadline

    A "W" on your transcript means you withdrew from the class. It doesn't hurt your GPA, but too many can raise questions. Dropping before this deadline means no record of it at all. Talk to your advisor before dropping any course.
  • November
    Course registration happens in November. You must meet with your academic advisor before you can register — this is required, not optional. Schedule that meeting early because advisor slots fill up fast.
    ⚠️ Check the BU calendar: last day to drop a course WITH a "W" grade
    Summer internship applications start opening in November and December. The best opportunities close early — don't wait until spring.
  • December
    Finals season. Build a study schedule at least two weeks before exams start.
    ⚠️ Book travel home early — prices spike significantly as the holidays approach
    Dorm students must vacate for winter break. If you can't go home or home isn't a safe place, contact the Dean of Students Office — BU has options and will not leave you without support.
Spring semester
  • January
    BU holds a winter activities fair early in January — great if you missed SPLASH or want to try something new. Many summer on-campus job applications open in January, including orientation positions. If you want to stay in Boston over the summer, apply early.
    ⚠️ RA applications for the following year typically open in January
  • February
    Housing portal opens in late January or early February. Complete the application and pay a deposit to get a housing selection number. Missing this window means starting from the back of the line.
    ⚠️ Check the BU calendar: last day to drop a course without a "W" grade
  • March
    Housing selection happens in March — seniors first, then juniors, then sophomores. Gender-affirming and gender-neutral housing options have separate deadlines; check the BU Housing site.

    Mid-terms are typically in March. Check your grades and get ahead of anything slipping — there's still time to turn things around.
    ⚠️ Check the BU calendar: study abroad application deadlines (summer and fall programs)
  • April
    ⚠️ FAFSA renewal priority deadline is in early April — if you receive financial aid, this is critical
    ⚠️ Check the BU calendar: last day to drop with a "W" or go pass/fail

    The FAFSA is the federal form that determines your financial aid each year — you must renew it annually, it doesn't carry over automatically. Go to studentaid.gov and log in with your FSA ID. Missing this deadline can affect your aid for next year.
  • May
    Reading period and final exams in early May. Dining hall service ends at the close of finals. Book travel home early — prices go up fast near Commencement.
    ⚠️ Check the BU calendar for move-out deadlines — they differ for degree and non-degree students
Satisfactory Academic Progress (SAP) — protect your financial aid

To keep your financial aid, you must pass at least 75% of the credits you attempt and maintain at least a 2.0 cumulative GPA each year. This applies to Pathways Scholars and most students receiving aid.

If you fall below SAP, your aid can be suspended — but you can appeal. The appeal asks you to explain what happened and what you'll do differently. You can include letters from doctors, professors, or advisors. If you're worried about your GPA or credits, talk to your advisor now, not at the end of the semester.

Learn more about SAP and how to appeal → bu.edu/finaid
